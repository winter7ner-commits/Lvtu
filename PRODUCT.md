# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Lvtu serves people who need legal help, lawyers who respond to and manage service orders, and administrators who operate the platform. Users are often trying to move through sensitive legal tasks with clear guidance: finding lawyers, browsing legal articles, creating consultation or service orders, tracking progress, handling payments, submitting results, reviewing evaluations, and managing platform content or disputes.

## Product Purpose

Lvtu is a full-stack lawyer service platform that connects legal-service demand with qualified professional support. Success means users can quickly understand the next step, complete forms without confusion, trust the status of an order, and see administrative workflows handled consistently across the user-facing app and the management console.

## Brand Personality

Trustworthy, efficient, professional. The interface should feel calm and capable rather than promotional, with language and interaction patterns that reduce uncertainty around legal-service tasks.

## Anti-references

No specific external anti-references yet. Avoid decorative legal cliches, flashy marketing treatment, unclear form flows, weak contrast, inconsistent button styles, and designs that make serious legal tasks feel casual or gimmicky.

## Design Principles

- Put the task before the decoration: every screen should make the next legal-service action obvious.
- Make trust visible through consistency: repeated controls, states, labels, and status language should behave the same across user and admin surfaces.
- Reduce anxiety in forms and order flows: preserve context, show progress clearly, and make validation messages actionable.
- Keep information scannable: prioritize dense but organized layouts for orders, lawyers, legal articles, evaluations, and admin records.
- Use professional restraint: primary color and motion should signal action or state, not ornament.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG AA. Maintain sufficient contrast for body text, placeholders, disabled states, and status labels; keep all interactive controls keyboard reachable and visibly focused; support reduced motion; do not rely on color alone for important states; and design legal forms so errors are specific, discoverable, and recoverable.
